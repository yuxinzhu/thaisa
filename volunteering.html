Thai Language Partners Program
The Thai Language Partners program (โครงการสนทนาภาษาไทย) is a collaboration between Thai language class at UCB and Berkeley Thai Students Association. It is an initiative that seeks to build a sense of community between Thai students from Thailand and Thai-Americans and other Americans who are interested in Thailand. The program gave them a unique opportunity to practice Thai conversation, connect with Thai students, learn about Thai culture, and join activities such as Loy Kratong, Song-gran, Thai nights etc. (In the past, some have even joined the ThaiSA's board!). In exchange, you may extend the time in order to practice English or ask them to help brush up your term paper. The program has been a smashing hit for participants in the past years. Now with the starting of the new semester, we are recruiting volunteers again.

 
  • Number of volunteers: 5 Thais to pair up with 5 third-semester students (Beginning Intermediate Thai 100A) and 8 Thais to pair up with 8 pairs of first-semester students (Introductory Thai 1A).

    •  
  • Frequency: Every other week. Total 5 times this semester.

    •  
  • Length of session: 20 minutes for Intemediate students. 10 or more minutes for Introductory students.

    •  
  • Place: Anywhere convenient for you. Avoid loud places.

    •  
  • Day: Any day and time to be arranged between you and your partners, as long as it occurs in the assigned week.

    •  
  • Task: Talking! (The conversation may be guided by the lesson and vocabulary learned in class). Answer questions on lessons.

    •  
  • Qualifications: (1) Enthusiasm for coaching language and sharing culture. (2) Empathy for people who have just learned Thai for 5 weeks or for 7 months. (3) Patience to speak slowly, and sometimes repetitively. (4) Ability to use simple words and to initiate conversation. (5) Willingness to resist the tendency to immediately defaulting to English when your partner do not understand you.

    •  
  • In Class Introduction: Beginning Intermediate class (Friday, September 20, from 12 noon to 12:30 pm at Wheeler Hall) and Introductory class (Wednesday, September 25, from 3:30 pm to 4:00 pm at Wheeler Hall)

    •  
  • Application or questions: Email Aajaan Supatra ASAP.
